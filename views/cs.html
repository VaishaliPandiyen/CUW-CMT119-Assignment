Brendan Eich

Brendan Eich created the JavaScript prototype in ten days in 1995 (Hoffman, B. 2019). What he started as a necessity - helping Netscape build a "new scripting language...that could be used to enhance the mostly static features of HTML and let developers move things around the page" led to the discovery of the third language of the web (Hoffman, B. 2019). He created one of the most powerful languages known for its performance and omnipresence and the potential to tap into many popular industries like Machine Learning and Data Analysis (Mohan, M. n.d.).

As Cassel (2019) said, Eich witnessed the birth of a standard and innovated on the evolving standards of how people wrote webpages. He played to the need at the right time, with an entire canvas to paint on.

"Its ascent to the most popular programming language is synonymous with the rise of the internet itself."
(DeGroat, T.J. 2019)

In 1998, Eich founded the Mozilla Project along with Jamie Zawinski to manage the open-source contributions to Netscape's source code. After Netscape was closed, he helped create the Mozilla Foundation (dunp.it n.d.), a global non-profit that "works to ensure the internet remains a public resource that is open and accessible to us all" (Mozilla Foundation, n.d.). The first JavaScript engine- SpiderMonkey, which Eich developed for Netscape, is still used in the Firefox browser (Wikipedia, 2022). He played a significant role in forming a foundation and project that stands for internet accessibility, cyber democracy and customer privacy.

From then till now, Eich was and is active in the emerging tech atmosphere where there is always a scope for first mover advantage. In 2015, he founded a privacy-centric browser- Brave, which blocks ads and tracking cookies . The browser uses a blockchain-based system called Basic Attention Token (BAT) to track media consumers' time and attention on websites (Hayes, A. 2022).

"When an advertiser signs up for a campaign, Brave uses 70% of the fee to purchase Basic Attention Tokens (BAT) from the open market and distribute them to users who have opted-in to the ads program."
(Khalili, J. 2022)

In an interview with Tyson (2022), Eich spoke about how online ads led to surveillance, "looting users and publishers", and malware distribution. Although this was not an intended outcome of their work, he founded Brave as a solution by putting the user first. He has developed a browser to browse without ads/ advertisers paying the users instead of platforms.
